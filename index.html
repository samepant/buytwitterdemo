Vote Yes for Twitter to explore a cooperative future

We are shareholders and users invested in Twitter, “the people's news network.”

↯ ↯ ↯

But with its stock price falling so far from expectations, we believe Twitter’s value isn't reflected in its current ownership model.

Twitter needs to reward investors and secure its future. A new ownership model could be the way.

That’s why we propose Twitter study how it might sell to its users, through a co-op or hybrid model. This study will help Twitter find ways to win user loyalty and reward investors. Twitter could follow REI, Associate Press, and other cooperative models that succeed by engaging their stakeholders through broad-based ownership and accountability mechanisms.

Our proposal is now up for a vote

Now until May 22, shareholders will vote on our proposal for Twitter to study “An Exit to Democratic User Ownership” with new governance and accountability.

To win, we need 50% or more Yes votes. Even with 3%, we can learn and re-submit next year.

A critical mass of Twitter shareholders want to see the company explore new models.

Perhaps you’re one of them?

Why vote yes?

  1. 1

    Democratic ownership has a proven record of adaptability and sustainability — REI, Associated Press, Green Bay Packers, these are examples of large cooperative enterprises that thrive due to their nature of being owned by their community of consumers, users, and fans.

  2. 2

    Studying new user ownership models may show potential alternatives where both shareholders and users stand to gain — users may be willing to adopt a paid subscription model for a service that they trust and love.

  3. 3

    When users have a stake in the management of an enterprise, they could provide direct input on their needs, which leads to new and improved features — that can lead to more user loyalty and simply make the product better over all.

Organize with us

This proposal emerged from a community of shareholders and users invested in Twitter’s future.

We began organizing around #WeAreTwitter and #BuyTwitter, following reports last Fall that Twitter was up for sale. Many of us feared Twitter’s new owner might ruin “the people’s news platform” with a narrow pursuit of profit or political gains.

More than 300 shareholders and 3,400 users have been organizing online to develop creative options for the company than corporate acquisition. Several experts have joined our effort, including Jim McRitchie, publisher of corpgov.net.

In December, we submitted a shareholder resolution for Twitter to explore its cooperative future. Now, we urge you to vote Yes on our proposal and help us mobilize more shareholders.
